The Boy Who Mastered The Pull Shot

Rohit Gurunath Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Nagpur, Maharashtra, is widely regarded as one of the greatest opening batsmen of all time. Known for his elegant timing, effortless stroke play, and a lethal pull shot, Rohit's journey wasn't an overnight success.

He started as an off-spinner who could bat a bit, but his coach Swami Dinesh Lad noticed his batting potential and promoted him to open the innings in school cricket. After making his international debut in 2007 and being a part of the inaugural T20 World Cup-winning squad, he faced years of inconsistency.

The turning point came in 2013 when MS Dhoni asked him to open in the Champions Trophy. The rest is history. He went on to score three double centuries in ODIs, including a monumental 264—the highest individual score in ODI history. As a captain, he transformed the Mumbai Indians into the most successful IPL franchise and led Team India to ultimate World Cup glory.
